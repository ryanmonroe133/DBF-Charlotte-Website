The Competition

Every year, more than 145 universities from around the world compete for the top place at the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics Design-Build-Fly competition. The goal is to design, build, and demonstrate the flight capabilities of an electric-powered, remote-controlled airplane that meets rigorous technical and mission requirements involving a commercial or military challenge.

Meet Your 2026-2027 Team

Ryan Monroe - Project Manager

Ryan is a senior majoring in Mechanical Engineering Technology, concentrating in Electromechanical Systems. He has held a variety of roles in engineering, including working for Collins Aerospace as a Product Support Intern, working for the Autonomous Robots and Systems Laboratory (ARSL) at UNC-Charlotte as an Autonomous Surface Vessel field researcher, and has accumulated 200+ hours in aviation maintenance as an A&P apprentice for AGL Aviation Services, primarily working on various Cessna and Mooney aircraft. Ryan has been involved with DBF UNC-Charlotte since its inception in 2025, and also serves as the AIAA UNC-Charlotte Student Chapter President.

History & Vision

Design Build Fly at UNC-Charlotte launched in 2025 with 5 engineering students. They competed for the first time in Tucson, Arizona by building a model X-1 aircraft. In 2026, the team doubled in size to 10 students, and competed with an all-3D-printed aircraft in Wichita, Kansas, placing 50th out of 98 teams. Building upon the previous successes of the past two teams, Design Build Fly at UNC-Charlotte aims to place in the top 25% of teams in 2027.
